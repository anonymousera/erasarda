Deep Learning Representation Learning

Representation Learning for Spatiotemporal Physical Systems

Pretraining and evaluation of encoder architectures (V-JEPA, ConvEncoder variants) to learn latent representations of active matter systems. Uses self-supervised objectives with frozen linear probes for downstream regression tasks; supports Slurm HPC distributed training via Hydra configuration management.

NLP Policy LLM-as-a-judge Evaluation

Animal Welfare Policy Tracker

Automated pipeline for classifying and ranking US state legislation on animal welfare. Queries the OpenStates API, applies four Hugging Face text classifiers (effect, advocacy, influence, trust) for weighted scoring, and optionally uses a local 8B instruction-tuned LLM to generate structured welfare assessments from bill text.

Big Data Hadoop Data Engineering

NYC Tourist Experience Analytics

End-to-end big data pipeline on the Hadoop ecosystem (HDFS, MapReduce, Hive) integrating hotel reviews, restaurant inspections, MTA subway ridership, and NYPD arrest data. Produces a composite Tourist Satisfaction Index aggregated at the ZIP-code level, with Tableau visualizations.

Applied Statistics Development Economics

Urban Road Access and Service Reachability in Ethiopia

Statistical analysis of the Ethiopia Urban Expansion Initiative household survey (8 cities, 4,000 households). Applies Mann-Whitney U tests to compare travel times to workplaces, markets, schools, and clinics between residents within 500m of new arterial roads versus control areas, stratified by gender.

LLM-as-Judge Evaluation AI Safety

Bedtime Story Generator with LLM-as-Judge

Multi-stage pipeline for generating age-appropriate bedtime stories (ages 5-10) combining safety checks with iterative quality improvement. An input guard screens requests before generation, and a panel of three LLM judges with distinct evaluation stances critique drafts against safety and quality preferences through a bounded revision loop, with full traceability of each evaluation iteration.

Notes & Resources

Things I found useful for standardized tests and grad school applications. Happy to share more on research internships abroad; feel free to reach out.

GRE Prep 327 / 340

All official ETS material. For vocabulary, 500–800 words is enough; choose high-frequency words rather than trying to cover everything. I used Barron's 333 High Frequency Words and revised daily, noting any words that came up in official practice questions. Key technique: form your own answer before looking at the options. The choices are designed to mislead; the reverse approach makes that worse.

TOEFL Prep 105 / 120

This LinkedIn post follows the old TOEFL pattern but has solid resources. Use all free material on the ETS website, including the ~20 sample writing questions with official scoring rubrics. If you've done GRE prep, schedule TOEFL shortly after; the verbal practice carries over more than you'd expect.
